Chapter 7.4: Covalent Bonding and Molecular Orbitals

Unlike ionic structures where valence shells exchange electron structures completely, covalent configurations achieve atomic parity through mutual orbital sharing. The electronegativity differential (Δχ) between constituent elements determines the dipole distribution across the bond vector.

When Δχ remains beneath a critical threshold of approximately 1.7, the compound exhibits non-polar characteristics. Sigma (σ) bonds represent localized orbital overlaps along the primary internuclear vector, whereas pi (π) bonds arise via parallel p-orbital arrays situated above and below the bonding axis plane.
