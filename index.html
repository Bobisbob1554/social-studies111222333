Chapter 7.4: Covalent Bonding and Molecular Orbitals

Unlike ionic structures where valence shells exchange electron structures completely, covalent configurations achieve atomic parity through mutual orbital sharing. The electronegativity differential determines bonding character and molecular geometry.

When Δχ remains beneath a critical threshold of approximately 1.7, the compound exhibits non-polar characteristics. Sigma (σ) bonds represent localized orbital overlaps along the internuclear axis, while pi (π) bonds involve parallel orbital interactions perpendicular to this axis.
